What to ask your makeup artist before you book

By Laveena · Notes from the chair · 6 min read

Booking a bridal makeup artist is one of those decisions that looks small on the checklist and turns out to be enormous on the day. Your flowers are in the background of your photos. Your face is in every single one. After years in the chair with brides across Lucknow and beyond, here are the questions I genuinely wish every bride would ask me, and every artist she is comparing.

1. "Have you worked with my skin, my tone, my ceremonies?"

Every face is different, and so is every wedding. A Hindu wedding with haldi in the morning and pheras at night is a different endurance test than a nikah and walima across two days. Ask your artist what ceremonies she has actually painted through. An artist who has done both will plan your base differently, and she will tell you exactly how without hesitating.

2. "What base will you use on me, and why?"

Not "which brand", which is what everyone asks, but which technique. Airbrush, HD cream, or a hybrid: each behaves differently on different skin types and in different seasons. A Lucknow summer wedding and a Delhi winter reception need different answers. If the answer is the same for every bride, that is a red flag. (I wrote a whole piece comparing airbrush and HD bases here.)

3. "How long will it actually last?"

"The makeup lasted nine to ten hours." That is not my claim. That is a bride, in her own words, the morning after.

Ask for real answers, and better, real evidence: messages, reviews, photos from the end of the night rather than the start. Any look survives the first hour. You are paying for hour nine.

4. "What happens if my schedule slips?"

Weddings run late. Baraats arrive when they arrive. Ask your artist how she handles a timeline that moves: does she build buffer into your slot, will she stay for a touch-up before the reception, does she leave you a touch-up kit? The answer tells you whether she has lived through real weddings or just portfolio shoots.

5. "Do you travel, and what does it cost?"

If your functions are split between cities, or your wedding is a destination one, ask early. A professional will quote travel transparently upfront rather than surprising you later. I travel from Lucknow to anywhere the wedding goes, and the quote always says so in plain numbers.

6. "Can we do a trial?"

A trial is not an upsell, it is a rehearsal. It is where we discover that the lipstick you loved on Instagram fights your outfit, or that your skin drinks up a certain primer. If a trial is possible for you, take it. If distance makes it hard, a detailed video consultation with photos of your outfits gets us most of the way.

The one question you should not need to ask

"Will you make me look like myself?" The answer should be visible in the artist's portfolio: brides who look like radiant versions of themselves, not copies of one face. That is the standard I hold my chair to, and it is the standard you deserve, whoever you book.
