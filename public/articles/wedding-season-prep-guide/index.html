The wedding-season prep guide for Hindu & Muslim brides

By Laveena · Notes from the chair · 7 min read

Wedding season in Lucknow is not one event, it is a season of seasons: haldi and mehndi, nikah and walima, pheras and receptions, sometimes all in one family in one month. Hindu and Muslim brides walk different ceremonial paths, and a smart prep timeline respects both. Here is the countdown I share with my own brides, whichever aisle, mandap or stage you are headed for.

Eight weeks out: skin first, always

Makeup amplifies the skin it sits on. Start now: a simple cleanser, moisturiser and sunscreen routine, water like it is your job, and one gentle exfoliation a week. Introduce nothing new after week four: no first-time facials, no untested actives. Wedding week is not the time to discover an allergy.

Six weeks: the DIY window

This is where tradition genuinely helps. The classic ubtan (besan, a pinch of turmeric, milk or rosewater) is a gentle weekly brightener generations of brides swear by. Rules: patch-test on your inner arm first, keep turmeric minimal unless you enjoy a yellow tint, and stop all DIY masks one week before the first ceremony.

For Hindu brides: pacing the marathon

  • Haldi is turmeric on skin by design. Book it at least two days before the wedding so any staining settles, and keep makeup that day minimal and waterproof.
  • Mehndi night photographs your hands more than your face, but reels happen: a soft glam with a strong lip carries the evening.
  • Pheras are long, warm and close-up: this is the airbrush day. Red and gold outfits love a classic winged eye and a red that leans blue, not orange.
  • Vidaai will involve tears. Waterproof liner, tubing mascara, and a dab-do-not-wipe rule keep the look intact through them.

For Muslim brides: elegance across two days

  • Nikah looks are often softer and more luminous: skin-first glam, a nude-rose lip, and lashes that read graceful under the dupatta rather than dramatic. If you will keep the dupatta pinned high, we plan the hairline and jhoomar placement together.
  • Walima is the night to turn the volume up: deeper eyes, a richer lip, and a base built to survive hours of greetings and photographs.
  • Praying with makeup on? Tell your artist: we can plan a look that respects wudu timing on the day.
Different rituals, same physics: heat, tears, hugs and ten hours of photographs. Prep beats panic, every single season.

Wedding week: the do-not list

No new products. No sun. No last-minute threading the day before (give brows 48 hours to calm). Sleep is a highlighter no brand sells. And confirm your trial look with your artist, in photos, in daylight and in indoor light, before the week begins.

The day itself

Eat before the chair. Bring your outfit dupatta for colour-matching. Hand your phone to a cousin. Then let go: this is the part where the look holds and you live your day.
