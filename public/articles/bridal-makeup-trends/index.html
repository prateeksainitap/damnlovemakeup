This season's bridal makeup trends, decoded honestly

By Laveena · Notes from the chair · 6 min read

Every wedding season arrives with a fresh set of trends, and every season half of them flatter real brides while the other half only flatter the camera. As someone who paints faces for a living, here is my honest read on what is trending right now, and what I actually recommend from the chair.

Glass skin, the honest version

The dewy, lit-from-within finish continues to rule. What the tutorials skip: true glass skin on a ten-hour wedding day is a maintenance commitment. The wearable version is a satin-glow hybrid: airbrushed base for hold, cream highlighter only on the high points, powder only where you crease. You get the light without the 6 PM shine.

Soft glam is the new heavy bridal

The biggest shift I see in Lucknow brides: away from full-coverage mask looks, toward skin that still looks like skin: diffused eyes, blurred-edge lips, brows brushed up rather than drawn on. It photographs beautifully and, more importantly, your groom recognises you.

Bold lip, bare everything else

For sangeet and cocktail nights, the one-hero-feature look is everywhere: a serious red or berry lip, barely-there eyes, groomed brows. It is fast, chic, and survives dinner better than a full smoky eye. Pair it with a proper transfer-proof formula and you are untouchable.

Trends are suggestions. Your face, your outfit and your ceremony are the brief.

The floating liner and graphic accents

Editorial liner shapes are all over social feeds. My take: gorgeous for pre-wedding shoots where we control the lighting, risky for the wedding day itself where tradition, jewellery and close relatives all have opinions. Save the experiments for the shoot; keep the pheras timeless.

Regional royalty: the comeback of tradition

Awadhi and Nawabi-inspired styling: real gold tones, classic kohl, sleek centre-parted hair with a strong maang tikka, is having a deserved revival. It suits Lucknow weddings like nothing imported ever will. If your outfit is traditional, resist the urge to fight it with a trendy face.

The DIY corner: what is safe to try at home

  • Lip scrub: sugar and honey, the night before any big lip look.
  • Brow lamination effect: a clean spoolie and a swipe of clear soap or brow gel, brushed up and set.
  • Setting-spray sandwich: mist, makeup, mist. The oldest trick that still beats most new launches.
  • Skip at home: face-taping, DIY peels, and anything a reel promises will transform you overnight.

The only trend that never dates

A base matched to your actual neck, brows that respect your bone structure, and a lip you can drink chai in. Book the artist who tells you what suits you, not just what is trending. That is the whole secret.
